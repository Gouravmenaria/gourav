HTML: The Foundation of Web Pages

HTML acts as the skeleton of web pages, defining the structure and content. It comprises a series of elements that denote different parts of a webpage, such as headings, paragraphs, images, links, and more. With its simple yet powerful syntax, HTML provides the framework upon which information is organized and presented on the internet.

CSS: Elevating Design and Presentation

CSS complements HTML by adding style and visual appeal to web pages. It allows developers to control the layout, colors, fonts, and overall aesthetics of a website. This separation of content (HTML) and presentation (CSS) streamlines development, enabling easier maintenance and flexibility in design modifications.
